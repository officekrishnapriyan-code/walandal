Water: From Source to Tap

We design IoT-enabled water solutions that continuously monitor quality and availability ‚Äî from elevated tanks and pipelines to community taps. Chlorine dosing, leak detection, and safety thresholds are managed with clear, actionable alerts.

  • Real-time monitoring of pH, turbidity, TDS, and chlorine.
  • Smart dosing logic to reduce risk of contamination.
  • Dashboards that work for both utilities and small communities.

Air: Making the Invisible Visible

Air quality is often ignored until it becomes a crisis. wal and al builds sensor networks and analytics that bring real-time air data into everyday decisions for buildings, campuses, and cities.

  • Indoor and outdoor sensor grids with edge intelligence.
  • Early warnings when comfort or safety limits are exceeded.
  • APIs and dashboards for planners, operators, and researchers.

Land: Listening to Ecosystems

Soil, vegetation, and habitats tell a story about resilience. Using robotics and distributed sensing, we help capture the data behind that story so that restoration, agriculture, and infrastructure can adapt faster.

  • Robotic and handheld sensing for soil and habitat health.
  • Spatial maps that combine field data with satellite insights.
  • Decision tools for planning interventions and monitoring impact.

Example Initiatives

wal and al works as an applied engineering partner, turning ideas into field-ready pilots. Here are example concepts that capture how we think and build.

Aqua Insight ‚Äì Intelligent Water Tank Monitoring

A monitoring and control layer for overhead tanks in rural and urban communities, combining sensors and cloud analytics to keep drinking water safe and available.

  • Continuous tracking of chlorine, pH, turbidity, and TDS.
  • Automatic alerts when thresholds drift into risk zones.
  • Evidence for better operation, maintenance, and policy decisions.

Resilient Microgrids & Energy-Aware Control

A software and data layer for microgrids that serves pumps, treatment units, sensors, and buildings using renewable energy in a resilient, predictable way.

  • Load-aware scheduling that protects critical services.
  • Integration of weather, storage, and demand forecasts.
  • Dashboards for operators and community stakeholders.

Robotic Field Sensing for Ecosystems

Autonomous and semi-autonomous platforms that map environmental conditions over time, giving planners and conservationists a living picture of their landscapes.

  • Mobile sensing for soil moisture, vegetation, and microclimate.
  • Repeatable routes for before/after comparisons.
  • Data products ready for GIS, modeling, and reporting.
